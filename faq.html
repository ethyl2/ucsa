FAQ

What is UCSA?

UCSA is Utah County Single Adults, the official organization for single adults age 46 and up of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, in Utah County.

Any mid-singles age 31-45 that want to participate with us are also welcome.

UCSA is under Priesthood leadership of the Area Authority Seventy & a Coordinating Council of representatives from stakes and wards throughout Utah County.

What is the purpose of UCSA?

The purpose of our organization is to minister to the over 25,000+ single adults of Utah County's 125+ stakes by providing single adult-specific, uplifting, spiritual and social events.

Coordinating Councils

Utah County is divided into areas - each area is made up of 8-12 stakes.

There is a Coordinating Council (CC) for each area.

Each Coordinating Council has:

  • An Agent Stake President (A stake from the area is chosen to preside over the Coordinating Council.)
  • 2 Coordinating Council Representatives (who communicate between the UCSA committee and their Coordinating Council)

Stakes

Each stake has leaders called to serve single adults.

  • Stake President
  • Relief Society Presidency members and Elders Quorum presidency members
  • Stake Single Adult Committee Representatives

Wards

Each ward has leaders called to serve single adults.

  • Bishop
  • Relief Society Presidency members and Elders Quorum presidency members
  • Ward Single Adult Representatives
